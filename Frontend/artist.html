ARPAN BHOWMIK

Arpan Bhowmik recreates themes of cityscapes and everyday life. Using a black and white base with a simple pop of colour, the artist shows the beauty of the city on his canvas. His paintings invoke strong feelings of nostalgia and belonging, that it is bound to draw the viewer deep into the canvas. Acrylic paint is used to give an effect of watercolour.

ANITA KUMAR

Anita grew up in an environment very close to nature. This has inspired her heavily to make vivid landscapes. Bright pop of colours with texture effect form a striking part of her work. She truly believes in the idea of humans deep connection with nature and tries to paint beautiful moments through her canvas.

CHANDRAKANT TAJBIJE

Shiva’s joy and delight is Nandi, his bovine mount. No animal in the sacred myths & folklore of India is more adored and revered than this elegant icon dating back to Harappan seals, Ajanta-Ellora frescoes, rock sculptures and temple icons welcoming the faithful from Kashmir to Kanyakumari. A bedrock of eternal values upholding the four pillars of faith: Sathya (truth), Dharma (Righteousness), Shanti (Peace) and Prema (Love), Nandi is the most beloved symbol of purity, patience and piety. Its elegant and stylized form is seen in all Indian folk art traditions like Bhil, Gond, Pichwai , Patta and even in abstract works by modern artists. Chandrakant Tajbije has created a brilliant blend of tradition with modernity using folk, tantric and symbolic forms in a pictorial style that bursts with life. He makes imaginative use of bold colour schemes and textures in vibrant acrylic that cast a spell on the viewer. Chandrakant’s “Nandi” is not only a joy to behold but an inspired invocation of the power of innocence, love and piety. In Seeing is Feeling & Being!

DILIP CHAUDHURY

Dilip Chaudhury’s works depict the Bengali countryside and his hometown Kolkata bathed in the monsoon rains. His black and white works are ever popular though of late he has been dabbling in color. Dilip Chaudhury has been painting various scenes from everyday life of Calcutta & sometimes Banaras, in watercolor. From ancient monuments, streets & ghats, to a rickshaw puller in action, he’s captured it all, beautifully. To somebody who has even a remote connection with Calcutta, he manages to bring a wave of nostalgia, as he symbolizes the character of the city of joy in his work.

DNYANESHWAR MANE

Dnyaneshwar’s unique mastery is in painting detailed carving work on canvas. Brought up in a highly cultured background, his paintings are heavily influenced by Indian culture, tradition and religion and the same is depicted in his art. Mane prefers to chose the simple life and hence lives in a quiet town of Maharasthra from where he produces his lovely paintings. All his paintings are a work of imagination wherein he combines the various temple cultures of India into one painting.

GANESH PANDA

Ganesh Panda uses palette knife to describe the flavour of the natural world. In his paintings, every stroke is visible. His choice of vibrant and sparkling colors and knife patches etched so perfect, makes his artworks intriguing to own. Painting is all about the expression of the artist’s various emotions like, joy, sorrow, lust, pain, passion, desire and insight into the society and his ability to depict them on canvas. Ganesh Panda, a painter par excellence, has achieved this expertise in his art. He is one of the most sensitive painters of the contemporary painting scenario. The setting sun, the changing autumn leaves, the ebbs and flows of a river – all form the central theme of Ganesh’s artowrk Each of his paintings brings different moods, colors and emotions. They are the true reflections of his feelings at the time of painting. His style is distinctly recognizable and demands the attention of the viewer, giving them the feeling of being able to walk in his picture and experience his emotions.

GOVIND DUMBRE

Govind Dumbre loves painting mainly the scenes of rainy rural India. He has been known for his paintings as the way he projects the streets of Konkan (Coastal India). He also has to his credit the lovely rains of Mumbai shown in the form of eye catching paintings . It is difficult to try to capture nature but Govind feels he’s up for the challenge.

KUMAR UMESH

Umesh has been heavily influenced by Rangoli art – and in his work he tries to show similar texture and style but by using acrylic paint. Rajasthani architecture has also influenced his work immensely and that is his core panting subject – different facades, jharokhas, palaces, intricate entry ways all form a part of his paintings.

MANISH CHAVDA

The first experience that one will get upon looking at any of Manish Chavda’s paintings is immensely meditative. He paints on the theme of contemporary-landscapes depicting birds and nature in their pure and organic form. His style is simple yet boasts intricate layering and detailing. Using oil paint on canvas, Manish develops transparent textures of colour and his delicate use of brushstrokes can be visibly seen in these dreamy landscapes. Soft tones set the canvas and they are surely a delight to see.

PRASHANTT YAMPURE

His work mainly focuses on realistic art depicting traditional Indian forms and compositions/ The colours of his canvas are inspired from the colours of the land and are therefore very close to their Indian roots. His work is based on the mythological characters of the Indian plethora depicted through Indian art forms like Kathakali and other rich Indian art forms. Vishnu, Vishnu’s avatar like Krishna and Rama, Shankar in his Rudravatar and other mythological figures are frequently seen in his works. His work is characterised by true to life depiction of subjects, with great attention to their facial expressions, the tautness of their forms and fall of their costumes.
